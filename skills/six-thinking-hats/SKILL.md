# Six Thinking Hats

De Bono's Six Thinking Hats is a structured **parallel thinking** method. All passes focus on the same hat at the same time — separating ego from idea and eliminating the conflation of facts, feelings, and judgement that derails most analysis.

> "Parallel thinking means that at any moment everyone is looking in the same direction." — De Bono

The Blue hat **facilitates**. The other five hats are **thinking directions**, not roles or personalities.

---

## The Six Hats

| Hat | Mode | Core Question |
|-----|------|---------------|
| 🔵 **Blue** | Process & facilitation | What thinking is needed? What's the agenda? What did we learn? |
| ⬜ **White** | Facts & data | What do we know? What is missing? What do we need? |
| 🔴 **Red** | Emotions & intuition | How does this feel? What is the gut reaction? What is the emotional signal? |
| 🟡 **Yellow** | Benefits & optimism | What are the benefits? Why could this work? What is the best case? |
| ⚫ **Black** | Risks & caution | What could go wrong? What are the weaknesses? What should we watch out for? |
| 🟢 **Green** | Creativity & alternatives | What alternatives exist? What new ideas arise? What haven't we tried? |

**Key rule:** Green is the *only* hat under which lateral techniques (PO provocations, random entry) are explicitly applied.

---

## Standard Hat Sequence

Blue opens and closes every session. The inner sequence is flexible; this default works well for most decisions:

```
OPEN:   🔵 Blue   — restate problem, set agenda, name the key question
        ⬜ White  — establish the fact base, surface gaps
        🔴 Red    — capture emotional signals (brief; no justification needed)
        🟡 Yellow — explore benefits and value with logical support
        ⚫ Black  — stress-test with disciplined critical scrutiny
        🟢 Green  — generate alternatives; apply PO and random entry
CLOSE:  🔵 Blue   — synthesise all five perspectives, name the next action
```

Blue may insert additional hat passes mid-session (e.g., a second Green pass to respond to risks the Black hat surfaced).

## Hat Prompts

### 🔵 Blue Hat — Opening

```
BLUE HAT — PROCESS & FACILITATION (Opening)

Problem/topic: [PROBLEM]

As the Blue hat facilitator:
1. Restate the problem clearly in one sentence.
2. Define what a good outcome for this session looks like.
3. Set the hat sequence (use the standard or adjust with justification).
4. Identify constraints: time, missing information, key stakeholders.
5. State the single most important question this session must answer.
```

### 🔵 Blue Hat — Closing

```
BLUE HAT — SYNTHESIS (Closing)

You have received perspectives from all five thinking hats.
Produce the session synthesis:

1. Key facts established (White hat summary — 2–3 bullets)
2. Emotional signals to respect (Red hat summary — 1–2 bullets)
3. Top 3 benefits identified (Yellow hat)
4. Top 3 risks identified (Black hat), with mitigations where known
5. Most promising creative ideas (Green hat — top 2–3)
6. Overall recommendation or decision
7. Next action: [WHAT] — Owner: [WHO] — By: [WHEN]
8. Further thinking needed? (if yes: what hat, what question)
```

### ⬜ White Hat — Facts & Data

```
WHITE HAT — FACTS & DATA

Problem: [PROBLEM]

Think only in facts and information. No opinions, no judgements, no feelings.

1. What do we know for certain? (List concrete facts with sources where possible)
2. What data or evidence supports each fact?
3. What information is missing that we need before deciding?
4. What information is available but hasn't been consulted yet?
5. What are the known unknowns?
6. What assumptions are being treated as facts that should be verified?

Output: A clean list of confirmed facts, data gaps, and information needs.
No opinions, feelings, or judgements — those belong to other hats.
```

### 🔴 Red Hat — Emotions & Intuition

```
RED HAT — EMOTIONS & INTUITION

Problem: [PROBLEM]

Express emotional reactions and gut feelings. No justification required.
These are signals, not verdicts. Red hat does not argue — it reports.

1. First instinctive reaction to this situation (one sentence):
2. What feels right about this?
3. What feels wrong or uncomfortable?
4. What is the gut feeling about the main options?
5. What emotional risk is present (fear, excitement, doubt, dread)?
6. If forced to decide right now, purely on instinct, what would the choice be?

Output: Brief, honest emotional signals. No analysis or explanation needed.
```

### 🟡 Yellow Hat — Benefits & Optimism

```
YELLOW HAT — BENEFITS & OPTIMISM

Problem: [PROBLEM]

Think constructively. Identify genuine value and benefits.
Yellow hat is not cheerleading — optimism must have logical support.

1. What are the key benefits of this approach or idea?
2. Why could this work? What makes it feasible?
3. What is the best realistic outcome if this succeeds?
4. Who benefits most, and how?
5. What existing strengths or assets support this?
6. What is the long-term upside?

Output: A substantiated list of benefits with brief reasoning for each.
```

### ⚫ Black Hat — Risks & Caution

```
BLACK HAT — RISKS & CAUTION

Problem: [PROBLEM]

Think critically. Identify risks, weaknesses, and reasons this could fail.
Black hat is the most valuable hat — it protects against costly mistakes.
It is not pessimism; it is disciplined caution in service of good decisions.

1. What are the main risks?
2. What could go wrong, and how likely / severe is each?
3. What are the weaknesses in the plan or idea?
4. What assumptions are being made that might be false?
5. What has gone wrong in similar situations before?
6. What would the strongest credible critic say?
7. What is the worst realistic outcome?

Output: A risk register. Include likelihood (High/Med/Low) and impact
(High/Med/Low) for each risk where possible.
```

### 🟢 Green Hat — Creativity & Alternatives

```
GREEN HAT — CREATIVITY & ALTERNATIVES

Problem: [PROBLEM]

Generate new ideas, alternatives, and possibilities. No judgement during
generation — PO statements and wild ideas are valid stepping stones.

Standard generation:
1. What alternatives exist that haven't been considered?
2. What would a completely different approach look like?
3. How could the Black hat risks be turned into opportunities?
4. What constraints could be removed or relaxed?

PO Provocation (apply at least one):
- REVERSAL:    PO: [reverse the key assumption about the problem]
- EXAGGERATION: PO: [push a key parameter to an extreme]
- DISTORTION:  PO: [change the sequence or relationship]
- RANDOM ENTRY: pick a random noun → list 5 attributes → force connections

For each provocation: move FORWARD — "If this were true, what would follow?"
Harvest any useful ideas the provocation surfaces.

Output: 5+ concrete alternative ideas or approaches. At least one must derive
from a PO provocation or random entry. Label the source of each idea.
```

## Modes

### Single-Agent Mode

One agent cycles through all hats sequentially. Fully commit to each hat's
perspective before moving to the next — no hedging or mixing hats.

**Procedure:**
1. State the problem clearly.
2. Work through each hat prompt in sequence (Blue open → White → Red → Yellow → Black → Green → Blue close).
3. Label each section clearly (`## ⬜ WHITE HAT`, etc.).
4. Do not carry judgements across hats (Black hat caution must not contaminate Yellow hat; both get a full, genuine pass).
5. Produce the structured output document (see `references/output-template.md`).

See `references/workflows.md#single-agent` for the complete prompt sequence.

### Multi-Agent Mode

Each hat is run by a separate spawned agent in parallel; a Blue hat orchestrator collects and synthesises.

**When to use:** Deep analysis in each hat, time-sensitive sessions needing parallel processing, or teams where independent perspectives matter.

**Procedure:**
1. Orchestrator (Blue hat) defines the problem and spawns five hat agents.
2. Hat agents run in parallel: White, Red, Yellow, Black, Green.
3. Orchestrator collects all responses.
4. Orchestrator runs Blue hat closing synthesis.
5. Optional: spawn a second Green agent using Black hat risks as additional input.

See `references/workflows.md#multi-agent` for `spawn_agent` calls and coordination pattern.

## Green Hat Techniques (Summary)

### PO — Provocative Operation

`PO` is a linguistic marker signalling a deliberate provocation — a stepping stone, not a proposal.

| Type | Pattern | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| Reversal | `PO: [reverse key assumption]` | `PO: customers set the price` |
| Exaggeration | `PO: [push parameter to extreme]` | `PO: the meeting lasts 5 seconds` |
| Distortion | `PO: [change sequence/relationship]` | `PO: testing happens before coding` |
| Random entry | `PO: [problem] is a [random word]` | `PO: the API is a garden` |

Always move **forward** from the provocation before evaluating. Then harvest.

### Random Entry (for creative blocks)

1. Pick a random noun (dictionary, nearby object, any noun).
2. List 5–8 attributes or associations of that word.
3. Force a connection between each attribute and the problem.
4. Harvest promising ideas.

See `references/hat-prompts.md#green-hat-extended` for worked examples.

---

## Key Rules

1. **Parallel thinking:** every pass wears the same hat — no mixing.
2. **Blue opens and closes** every session — no exceptions.
3. **Green is the only hat** where PO and random entry apply.
4. **Red needs no justification** — feelings are reported, not argued.
5. **Black is not pessimism** — it is disciplined caution protecting good decisions.
6. **Yellow must be substantiated** — optimism without logic is not Yellow hat.
7. **Always end with a next action** — Blue closing must name a concrete step.
